### Hi there 👋


My name is Martin and I'm a researcher at the [Lundbeck Centre for GeoGenetics](https://globe.ku.dk/research/geogenetics/) at the University of Copenhagen.

My background is in ancient DNA genomics. I did my PhD [Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology](https://www.eva.mpg.de/genetics/index/) in Leipzig where I worked on the evolutionary histories of modern humans, Neanderthals, and Denisovans. My PhD advisors were [Janet Kelso](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Janet_Kelso) and [Svante Pääbo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Svante_P%C3%A4%C3%A4bo). Before I moved to Leipzig to do my PhD, I did an undergraduate degree in computer science at Charles University in Prague.

In my current job, I’m working on spatiotemporal methods for population genetics, ecology and evolution. The most recent outcome of this work is an R package called [_slendr_](https://github.com/bodkan/slendr) which provides a framework for building programmable population genetic simulations on a real geographic landscape.  

Previously, I have developed an R package [_admixr_](https://github.com/bodkan/admixr), which is designed to build fully automated, reproducible analytic pipelines using the ADMIXTOOLS software. This package has already been used in [several dozen](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?oi=bibs&hl=en&cites=13286994334855947290) of publications and has thousands of users. Using ADMIXTOOLS normally requires a significant knowledge of shell scripting and text munging to accomplish even the simplest of tasks. Many users reported that the _admixr_ R package allowed them to perform complex analyses even though they have only minimal, if any, programming knowledge. Despite its quirks, R is truly an exceptional environment for building computational interfaces for data analysis, and is the main reason why it remains my favourite programming language.

You can find more information about me, my work, and about the ways to contact me on my [personal website](https://bodkan.net/).
